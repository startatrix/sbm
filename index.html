How to configure Smart Battery Software

Last updated May 19, 2023

Congratulations, and thank you for installing our state of the art software for your battery management. Now that you have our software installed on your laptop, you want to make sure to finalize the configuration and go through the following one time setup instructions in order to benefit from our 100% hands off charging feature and finalize the configuration. For the automated process to work,  you will need either a Smart plug or a Smart Wall Socket. If you do not have one, you can purchase one from the list of the devices listed below. Choose one that will work in your country. If you already have a similar SMART device, you can continue with the following steps to connect your smart device to our monitoring software.

Installing SmartLife app

The first step is to create a SmartLife account as this will be used later in the process. If you already have an account you can skip this step. If you do not have an account, scan the QR code on your phone and install the app. The next few steps will guide you to connect your smart device to the SmartLife system.

       

                     

Install the SmartLife app on your phone.

Create a SmartLife account

Now that you downloaded the SmartLife app, you need to login and add your device. If you don’t have an account yet, click on sign up and follow the steps below.

Enter your email and click the Get Verification Code button

Check your email and enter the verification code on this screen.

Choose a password for your account and click Save.

Your registration should be successful.

Click Go to App.

Adding a new device

Follow your smart plug manufacturer instructions, that shows how to make the smart plug discoverable. Usually, this is done by pressing a button on the dice for a set period of time. For my device, I pressed the power up button for a few seconds so that the device could be found by the app. Different devices might have different methods to become discoverable. Please read the manufacturer details on how to do this for your device.

Click the Add Device button.

Device was discovered and can be added. Click Add button

You will be asked to choose the wifi connection to use so that the device can be available on the network. Click Next.

This shows that the device is being configured here.

The device was successfully added to the SmartLife app. Click Done to continue.

Shows all your configured devices on the main screen.

Testing your device

At this point you need to confirm that the device works and responds to the commands from this app.

Toggle the ON/OFF switch and check that your device

is behaving to the commands.

 

Proceed to the final step.

Linking them together

Now that we have configured your device with the SmartLife app, we need to complete the final step. This will let the SMART battery management software control your SMART device whenever the battery levels reach the low and high limits.

Search for Smart Battery Monitor on your windows and run it. When you open the Smart Battery Monitor application for the first time, you will see a similar layout as shown below.

Click on the Device Tab.

Click on the Sign-In button and enter your email and password you entered for your SmartLife app earlier on when you created the account. Please note that these details are not stored on your machine but used for authentication purposes.

If you entered the correct details, the login window will be closed and the Select Device button will be enabled. Click on the Select Device  to continue.

A window showing your configured device on SmartLife should appear in the list.

If you configured more than one device, they will appear in the list. Click on the item you want to connect. The Select button should become enabled. Click on the Select button to choose this device. Click on the On button and the device should turn on. Click on the Close button.

The device selected will be displayed here.

Click the Dashboard Tab. You will see that your battery is being charged now. Once the battery level for the high limit is reached the device will automatically turn off.
